Method

The 4:6 method begins by dividing the total water into 40% and 60%. You pour the first 40% in two pours, and then decide how many pours you want to make for the last 60%.

The 40%

The ratio of the first 2 pours (40%), determine the balance of sweetness and acidity. When the first pour is greater than the second pour, the final cup tastes more acidic. When the first pour is less than the second pour, the cup tastes of more sweetness.

The 60%

The remaining number of pours (60%) will decide the strength of the coffee. A fewer number of pours will introduce less agitation to the coffee bed and the final cup will taste less strong. A larger number of pours will introduce more agitation to the coffee bed (increasing extraction), and the final cup will taste stronger.
